NLP (Natural Language Processing) Concepts

Overview

NLP (Natural Language Processing) is a growing subfield in Computer Science. In simple terms, NLP is the interaction between humans and AI (Artificial Intelligence) where human languages are processed and analyzed by computers. People all around the world use many NLP applications in their daily life. For example, Siri, Alexa, Google Translate, Grammarly, etc. are all built using NLP. It is very difficult to program computers to understand human languages and process them. Since people ask Siri millions of different questions, developers have to program Siri accordingly and constantly add new data and regularly fix new bugs in order to satisfy the users.

Language models are very complicated as there can be millions of possibilities for words and that the models adapt depending on the frequency of the user's choice. There are many types of language models and you can check out this resource to learn more.

N-grams are basically a sequence of n items from a given text. There are different types of n-gram language models, including unigrams, bigrams, trigrams, etc. The image above shows if each word is analyzed separately or together, which depends on the gram. In order to program language models, high-level math is required in order to calculate probabilities of vast amounts of data.
